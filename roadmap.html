The Roadmap

A phased approach that stops the bleeding first, fixes the foundation second, then scales what works. Each phase builds on the last.

Phase 1: Stop the Bleeding

Immediate Priority | Revenue Recovery Focus

Before building anything new, we stop the active losses. These are quick wins that recover revenue from your existing supporter base.

Failed Payment Recovery

Activate FundraiseUp's Card Account Updater ($0.25/card). This automatically updates saved payment methods when cards are replaced.

$100K+ Annual Recovery

Cancellation Triage

Build a system that cross-references cancellation signals with donor value. High-value supporters who cancel trigger personal outreach from leadership.

Retention Priority

Re-engagement Flows

For supporters who cancel: "Sorry to see you go" email sequence offering lower tiers or pause options instead of full cancellation.

Churn Prevention

Milestone Recognition

Send recognition at key thresholds: 1-year anniversary postcard, 2-year thank you, special status for long-term supporters. Supporters who reach 10+ months are much more likely to stay.

Loyalty Building

Phase 2: Website Rebuild

Foundation Work | Platform Migration

Move off Squarespace to a proper CMS. Nothing else scales until this is done. The website is the foundation for everything that follows.

Platform Migration

Move to a CMS that supports proper analytics, content management, and conversion optimization. Squarespace is a ceiling on growth.

Critical Foundation

Conversion Infrastructure

Fix GA4 event mapping. Implement UTM tracking on all links. FundraiseUp captures UTMs natively. Enable proper attribution.

Measurement Fix

Stories Hub

Consolidate thin /updates/ posts into evergreen regional authority pages. Burma, Afghanistan, Venezuela. Structure for SEO and engagement.

Content Architecture

YouTube Landing Page

Build a dedicated page for video-referred visitors: "You just watched this. Here's how to help." Unlock the 34,000 monthly YouTube visitors.

Conversion Path

Impact Transparency

Show exactly how funds are used. Add "what your donation does" impact equivalencies. "$50/month = 3 med packs in the field."

Trust Building

Social Proof

Add community language: "Join 10,300 people supporting rescue operations." Donors need to feel they are joining a movement.

Conversion Lift

Phase 3: Growth Channels

Acquisition Engine | Paid + Organic

With the foundation fixed, activate the channels that will drive new donor acquisition. Free channels first, then paid amplification.

Google Ad Grants

$10,000/month in free search ads for nonprofits. Requires conversion tracking to be fixed first. Captures high-intent searches.

$120K/Year Free Media

Email Strategy

Email is the #1 retention channel. Most current donors are NOT on social media. They came through podcasts. Build quality sequences.

Retention Focus

Social Media Paid

Boost field dispatches within 24 hours of posting. $50-100 targeting US adults 25-65 interested in humanitarian aid and veterans.

Amplification

SEO Content

Build intent-mapped pages targeting non-branded searches: "veteran charity," "Burma relief," "humanitarian evacuation."

Long-Term Growth

Referral Program

89% of supporters would recommend Stronghold. Give them a structured way to refer. "Cover Your 6" postcard packs.

Word of Mouth

Monthly State of the Union

Scripted monthly video update on what's happening. Feed through YouTube. Maintain engagement and provide shareable content.

Engagement

Phase 4: Scale

Paid Acquisition | Data-Driven Optimization

With audience intelligence from Phase 3, invest in paid search when you know who converts. Expand podcast partnerships. Build ambassador program.

Google PPC

Layer in paid search spend in Q4 when you have the audience intelligence to target efficiently. Year-end giving season.

Strategic Investment

Podcast Partnerships

47% of donors came from MrBallen. This is a single point of failure. Model new partnerships on that playbook.

Diversification

Ambassador Program

73 "super fans" have made 49+ donations each. Give them a formal role. Brand ambassadors, event hosts, referral champions.

Community Building

Major Donor Cultivation

One-time donors give 11x more per transaction. Build a separate funnel for major gift prospects. Personal cultivation.
